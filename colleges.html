1. Oregon State University (Cascades)

OSU Cascades is an extension of OSU, located on the west-side of Bend, Oregon. The school offers a computer science program with an applied option of web and mobile software development. The program is part of OSU's School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science in the College of Engineering. OSU Cascades is partnered with the MECOP, an organization that provides high quatlity internships for students.

Graduate School (in Corvallis)

The graduate level Computer Science programs that OSU offers include a Masters of Science, a Master's of Engineering, a Ph. D and a Model Ph. D. Students studying at the graduate level have the option of focusing on algorithms, computer systems and networking, human-computer interaction, programming languages, software engineering and artificial intelligence and machine learning. The graduate programs are only offered at OSU's Corvallis campus.

OSU's graduate Computer Science research covers a very broad spectrum, including 13 different topics. These topics include: Data Science and Engineering, Artificial Intelligence and Robotics, Electronic Materials and Devices, Integrated Electronics, Energy Systems, Cybersecurity, Communications and Signal Processing, Networking and Computer Systems, Software and Human-Computer Interaction, Programming Languages, Theoratical Computer Science, Health Engineering, and Computer Graphics and Vizualization.

2. Portland State University

Portland State University is locacted in in downtown Portland, Oregon. The school offers a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science from the Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science. The program focuses on the sofware end of CS. PSU participates in the MECOP program to help students find internships. PSU also offers their own internship program called the PCEP (PSU/PDX Co-Operative Educational Program) that partners with local businesses to give students real-life experince working in tech companies.

Graduate School

Graduate level programs for Computer Science at PSU include a Master's of Science, a Ph. D, and Certificate in Computer Security.

PSU has a pretty broad range of high level interesting research occuring at their school. Topics of research include: Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, Computer Graphics and Vision, Computer Science Education, Computer Security, Databases, Programming Languages, Software Engineering, and Systems and Networking. The Compter Graphics and Vision Lab at PSU has support from a few major tech companies including Google, Adobe, and Intel.
